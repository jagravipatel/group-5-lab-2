Animals

Chapter 1: Ocean Animals

  1. Blue Whale

    2. The Blue Whale is the largest animal to ever live, in the entire history of Earth. Reaching lengths of at least 110 feet (33 meters) and weights of 209 tons (190 tonnes), these animals are only slightly smaller than the United States Space Shuttle. Their incredible size is only possible because of their aquatic lifestyles and the buoyancy provided by seawater. On land, an animal as lare as the Blue Whale would almost certainaly be crushed under its own weight.



    Figure 1 : Blue Whale

  2. Whale Shark

    3. The largest fish in the world is the graceful, harmless whale shark. Though they reach the size of a school bus, Whale Sharls eat tiny plankton and fish eggs, which they filter feed as they swim slowly along with their giant mouths wide open. They are one of only three species of filter feeding sharks.


    Figure 2 : Whale Shark

  3. Great White Shark

    4. The larget predatory fish in the world, capable of eating marine mammals that weigh several hundred pounds - is the great White Shark. The only two fishes that grow larger than Great Whites are the Whale shark and the Basking Shark, both filter feeders that eat plankton.

    Figure 3 : Great White Shark

Chapter 2: Sky Animals

  1. Blue Jay

    2. Blue Jays, Latin name Cyanocitta cristata, are native to North America but are commonly found in the central and eastern regions of the United States. They are ten inches in length and weigh three ounces. Blue Jays eat nuts, seeds, and acorns, but they are known to eat small insects such as grasshoppers, catepillars, or beetles.These beautiful birds are recognizable by their bright blue colors, constrasting light grey chest and white face.


    Figure 4 : Blue Jay

  2. Red Crested Turaco

    3. The Red-Crested Turaco also known by the latin name Tauraco erythrolophus is native to Africa. They can be found in the Savanna, woodlandsm and forests of sub Saharan Africa. Red Crested Turacos are about 20 inches in length and weigh less than 1 pound. They eat seeds, fruits, flowers, leaves and termites. These birds are not the best flyers, but they can run extremely fast and they sound like a jungle monkey. They are green bodies, white-faced and have a red crest.


    Figure 5 : Red Crested Turaco

  3. Golden Pheasants

    4. Golden Pheasants, Latin name Chrysolophus pictus, are also called "Rainbow Pheasant" and "Chinese Pheasant". They are local to the mountains of the west and central china. Males can measure up to 3 feet and females up to 2 feet. They eat berries, grains, seeds and grubs. They are identified by their unique and beautifully golden crust, their feathers that range in a rainbow of colors, and long tail feathers.



    Figure 6 : Golden Pheasants

Chapter 3: Land Animals

  1. Tiger

    2. Being the largest wild cats and one of the strongest animals in the world, Tigers are in a league of their own. They are solitary hunters and prefer to stay alone. There are 5 different subspecies of tiger namely: Bengal, South China, Sumatran, Siberian and Indochinese. These masculine beasts are the biggest cats in the world, one of the deadliest predators and the most skilleddd as well. No wonder it tops our list right?


    Figure 7 : Tiger

  2. Lion

    3. Lions secure second spot on our list. Though often calles as the "King of the jungle". They are actually not found in jungles and they prefer a vast stretch of grasslands and plains. Its mighty roar can be heard even if you are 5 miles away! Not only that, lions can run up to 81kmph for a given stretch of land and can also leap as far as 35 to 40 feet.


    Figure 8 : Lion

  3. Maned Wolf

    4. Maned Wolves are often found in central South America, mainly in the grasslands of the south and Southeastern Brazil. Before you get cofused, maned wolves are not actually wolves.... nor foxes. In fact, they have more resemblance to dogs. They are solitary hunters and you would be surprised to know that half their diet consists of plants. Yes, aprat from hunting rabbits and armadillos, their diet is also based on eating fruits, roots and bulbs. So, they play the role of an important omnivore in nature.
